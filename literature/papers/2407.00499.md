## Abstract (verbatim)

"Uncertainty quantification (UQ) in natural language generation (NLG) tasks remains an open challenge, exacerbated by the closed-source nature of the latest large language models (LLMs). This study investigates applying conformal prediction (CP), which can transform any heuristic uncertainty notion into rigorous prediction sets, to black-box LLMs in open-ended NLG tasks. We introduce a novel uncertainty measure based on self-consistency theory, and then develop a conformal uncertainty criterion by integrating the uncertainty condition aligned with correctness into the CP algorithm. Empirical evaluations indicate that our uncertainty measure outperforms prior state-of-the-art methods. Furthermore, we achieve strict control over the correctness coverage rate utilizing 7 popular LLMs on 4 free-form NLG datasets, spanning general-purpose and medical scenarios. Additionally, the calibrated prediction sets with small size further highlights the efficiency of our method in providing trustworthy guarantees for practical open-ended NLG applications."

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

ConU turns *self-consistency* (the rate at which the LLM produces semantically equivalent answers across stochastic decodes) into a CP nonconformity score and applies split-CP to obtain prediction sets with **correctness coverage** guarantees on free-form NLG. The uncertainty condition is explicitly aligned with correctness — high self-consistency is treated as evidence the modal answer is correct — so the CP threshold controls a marginal correctness rate rather than a generic plausibility rate. The method is fully **black-box**: no logits, no internal states, just sampled completions and a semantic-equivalence judge. Result: small calibrated prediction sets across 7 LLMs and 4 NLG datasets including medical QA.

## Method

- **Conformity score**: a self-consistency-based uncertainty measure — for an input, sample K answers, cluster by semantic equivalence, score by frequency of the modal cluster (or related self-consistency statistic). Higher self-consistency = lower nonconformity.
- **Calibration set**: held-out (input, gold-answer) pairs; semantic-equivalence judgement converts gold-answer match into a {0,1} correctness label, which is used to set the CP threshold for correctness coverage.
- **Coverage statement**: marginal **correctness** coverage at level 1 - alpha — the prediction set contains a correct answer with probability at least 1 - alpha (not merely the model's modal answer).
- **Granularity**: **whole-sequence answer level** — one prediction set per query, containing answer strings.

## Reported results

- 7 popular LLMs (the abstract does not name them; likely include GPT-3.5/4, LLaMA-2, Vicuna, Mistral-class models given EMNLP 2024 timing).
- 4 free-form NLG datasets across general-purpose and medical scenarios (e.g., TriviaQA, NQ, MedQA-style; specific names not in abstract).
- Outperforms prior SOTA uncertainty measures on the calibrated UQ task; small prediction-set sizes at the same nominal correctness coverage.

## How CoT-CP positions against this

- **Overlap with CoT-CP**: Both yield correctness-coverage-style guarantees on free-form LLM output. Self-consistency is the obvious **baseline conformity score** for any sequence-level CP-for-LLM method. CoT-CP must report against ConU.
- **What CoT-CP adds**:
  1. **Step-level, not whole-answer-level.** ConU calibrates over full answers; CoT-CP calibrates each reasoning step, allowing per-step abstention. For long chains the difference is large: a single bad step does not require resampling the whole answer.
  2. **Selective accuracy on chains, not on single-shot answers.** ConU's set-of-answers framing is poor for math / proof chains where the answer is a single token but the *chain* is the artifact under audit.
  3. **Sample-efficient.** Self-consistency requires K samples per query (typically K = 10-40); CoT-CP needs only one chain plus a per-step verifier, which can be cheaper at long chain length.
  4. **Compatible with chain-as-artifact use cases**: tool-use traces, agent plans — where ConU's answer-clustering does not even apply.
- **Required experimental baseline**: yes — ConU is the **canonical sequence-level black-box-LLM CP baseline**. Our table MUST include a row "ConU (self-consistency, sequence-level)" at matched alpha and budget K.
- **Required citation**: yes, **section 2 (related work)** and as a baseline row in experiments. ConU is also a useful citation for the "correctness coverage" framing — we should adopt their terminology.
