## Abstract (verbatim)

"Large language models have achieved impressive performance across diverse tasks. However, their tendency to produce overconfident and factually incorrect outputs, known as hallucinations, poses risks in real world applications. Conformal prediction provides finite-sample, distribution-free coverage guarantees, but standard conformal prediction breaks down under domain shift, often leading to under-coverage and unreliable prediction sets. We propose a new framework called Domain-Shift-Aware Conformal Prediction (DS-CP). Our framework adapts conformal prediction to large language models under domain shift, by systematically reweighting calibration samples based on their proximity to the test prompt, thereby preserving validity while enhancing adaptivity. Our theoretical analysis and experiments on the MMLU benchmark demonstrate that the proposed method delivers more reliable coverage than standard conformal prediction, especially under substantial distribution shifts, while maintaining efficiency. This provides a practical step toward trustworthy uncertainty quantification for large language models in real-world deployment."

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

DS-CP applies **weighted / non-exchangeable CP** (Tibshirani 2019, Barber et al. 2022) to LLM prompts: it embeds each prompt in a sentence-embedding space and reweights calibration samples by **proximity to the test prompt**, so that domain-shifted test queries are calibrated against semantically nearby calibration points. The reweighting preserves finite-sample validity (under standard weighted-CP assumptions) and restores coverage that standard exchangeable split-CP loses under shift. Empirical demonstration is on **MMLU** with subject-level shift. The contribution is essentially "weighted CP with prompt-embedding similarity weights, instantiated for LLM QA."

## Method

- **Conformity score**: a standard LLM nonconformity score on the QA / classification answer (the abstract is silent on whether this is logit-based, self-consistency-based, or a softmax-style score; the framework is score-agnostic).
- **Calibration set**: a labelled calibration pool; each calibration sample is **reweighted** by an embedding-space similarity (kernel) to the test prompt before computing the conformal threshold.
- **Coverage statement**: weighted-CP marginal coverage at 1 - alpha under the proximity-reweighted distribution — provides coverage adaptivity under covariate shift, in the spirit of Tibshirani et al. (NeurIPS 2019).
- **Granularity**: **prompt / answer level** — the reweighting is per-prompt; the prediction set is over candidate answers (MMLU is multiple-choice).

## Reported results

- **Benchmark**: MMLU, with explicit subject-level / domain shift.
- **Models / numbers**: not provided in the abstract; the abstract reports qualitative gains ("more reliable coverage than standard CP, especially under substantial distribution shifts, while maintaining efficiency").
- The headline qualitative finding is *restored coverage under shift at minimal set-size cost*.

## How CoT-CP positions against this

- **Overlap with CoT-CP**: Both are inference-time post-hoc CP methods on top of black-box LLMs. DS-CP is the canonical *shift-aware* baseline that any CP-for-LLM paper claiming robustness should cite.
- **What CoT-CP adds**:
  1. **Step granularity vs. prompt granularity.** DS-CP reweights at the *prompt* level; it has no notion of intra-chain calibration. CoT-CP can reweight at *step* level, e.g., reweight calibration steps by similarity to the current reasoning step's content / topic.
  2. **Composable with DS-CP, not in conflict.** A natural CoT-CP variant inherits DS-CP's prompt-embedding reweighting at the chain entry, then layers step-level CP on top. This is a positive citation: DS-CP is *complementary*, not competing.
  3. **Selective-accuracy on chain answers, not multiple-choice.** DS-CP's MMLU evaluation is multiple-choice; CoT-CP targets free-form chain output where the unit-of-shift is more subtle (proof-style content, tool-use traces).
  4. **Beyond shallow proximity.** Sentence-embedding proximity is a shallow proxy; CoT-CP can use *step-graph* features (number of steps, chain entropy) as richer non-exchangeability weights.
- **Required experimental baseline**: yes if any CoT-CP claim involves robustness to shift (different math domains, OOD problem types, tool ecosystems). On in-distribution-only experiments, it is sufficient to cite without a head-to-head row.
- **Required citation**: yes, **section 2 (related work, distribution shift subsection)** and again whenever weighted / non-exchangeable CP is invoked. Also cite as the inspiration for any embedding-proximity reweighting we adopt.
