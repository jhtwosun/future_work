## Abstract (verbatim)

"Large Language Models (LLMs) frequently hallucinate, limiting their reliability in critical applications. Conformal Prediction (CP) addresses this by calibrating error rates on held-out data to provide statistically valid confidence guarantees. Recent work extends CP to LLM factuality to filter out risky claims, ensuring that hallucination rates remain below a user-specified level (e.g., 10%). While prior methods treat claims independently, Coherent Factuality extends to multi-step reasoning by representing outputs as dependency graphs and jointly validating claims with their logical ancestors. A key limitation is that Coherent Factuality is not differentiable, requiring hand-crafted scorers that at high reliability levels remove nearly 60% of true claims. We introduce Differentiable Coherent Factuality (DCF), a fully differentiable relaxation that enables learning improved scorers while provably recovering the original algorithm's guarantees. Experiments on two benchmark reasoning datasets demonstrate DCF achieves up to 141% improvement in claim retention while maintaining reliability guarantees, representing a significant step towards reliable conformal LLM systems."

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

DCF differentiates through the Coherent-Factuality CP procedure (Cherian/Mohri-style claim-graph filtering) so the *scorer* used to rank claims and reasoning steps can be **trained end-to-end** rather than hand-crafted. The differentiable relaxation provably recovers the original algorithm's guarantees in the limit, so trained scorers retain the user-specified hallucination bound (e.g., 10 percent). Headline empirical claim: up to 141 percent improvement in true-claim retention at matched reliability versus the non-differentiable Coherent-Factuality baseline. The method is **training-time** — the scorer is learned once and then used in a standard split-CP filter at inference.

## Method

- **Conformity score**: a *learned* differentiable scorer over claims / reasoning-graph nodes; trained via a differentiable relaxation of the Coherent-Factuality filter so that the surrogate loss aligns with conformal coverage.
- **Calibration set**: held-out reasoning chains annotated with claim-level correctness, used both (a) to train the scorer (via the differentiable relaxation) and (b) for the standard split-CP threshold at inference.
- **Coverage statement**: marginal coverage / hallucination-rate bound at user-specified level alpha (e.g., 10 percent), inherited from Coherent Factuality and provably preserved by the differentiable relaxation.
- **Granularity**: **claim-level inside a reasoning dependency graph** — claims are jointly validated with their logical ancestors, so the practical unit is a claim-node whose acceptance is conditioned on ancestor acceptance.

## How CoT-CP positions against this

- **Overlap with CoT-CP**: Both target factuality of multi-step LLM reasoning with CP-style coverage. DCF and CoT-CP both decompose the chain into nodes whose acceptance is calibrated. DCF is the strongest **claim-graph** competitor in the space.
- **What CoT-CP adds**:
  1. **Inference-time post-hoc, no retraining.** DCF requires training a scorer; CoT-CP uses a fixed split-CP procedure on top of an off-the-shelf API LLM. This is a deployment / cost / black-box-model differentiator: CoT-CP is applicable to GPT-4-class APIs that we cannot fine-tune; DCF is not.
  2. **Step granularity, not claim-graph granularity.** CoT-CP calibrates at the *semantic reasoning step* (a sentence / proof line / equation), not at extracted-claim-graph nodes. Step-level is cheaper to extract, does not require an entailment-graph parser, and aligns with how humans audit a chain.
  3. **Per-step abstain / defer / replan**, exposed as a branching control. DCF is a one-shot filter; CoT-CP is a streaming gate over the chain.
  4. **No differentiable surrogate, no retraining instability.** DCF's relaxation only "provably recovers" guarantees in the limit; trained scorers may be brittle under shift. CoT-CP's split-CP is finite-sample distribution-free.
- **Required experimental baseline**: we MUST compare against DCF at matched alpha on the same benchmarks. If DCF is closed-source, we re-implement the differentiable relaxation on a small scorer; the comparison must control for "training-time vs inference-time" by also reporting compute cost.
- **Required citation**: yes, **section 2 (related work) and methods (when defining the step-level conformity score)**, plus a row in the experimental table. Position explicitly as "training-time differentiable claim-graph CP vs. inference-time post-hoc semantic-step CP."
