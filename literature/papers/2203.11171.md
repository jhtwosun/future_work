## Abstract (verbatim)

> Chain-of-thought prompting combined with pre-trained large language models has achieved encouraging results on complex reasoning tasks. In this paper, we propose a new decoding strategy, self-consistency, to replace the naive greedy decoding used in chain-of-thought prompting. It first samples a diverse set of reasoning paths instead of only taking the greedy one, and then selects the most consistent answer by marginalizing out the sampled reasoning paths. Self-consistency leverages the intuition that a complex reasoning problem typically admits multiple different ways of thinking leading to its unique correct answer. Our extensive empirical evaluation shows that self-consistency boosts the performance of chain-of-thought prompting with a striking margin on a range of popular arithmetic and commonsense reasoning benchmarks, including GSM8K (+17.9%), SVAMP (+11.0%), AQuA (+12.2%), StrategyQA (+6.4%) and ARC-challenge (+3.9%).

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

Self-consistency is the original "just sample $N$ chains and take the majority answer" recipe.
It replaces greedy decoding with temperature/nucleus sampling of $N$ independent reasoning paths $r^{(i)}$ that each terminate in an answer $a^{(i)}$, then aggregates by marginalizing over the *latent* reasoning variable: $\hat{a} = \arg\max_a \sum_{i=1}^{N} \mathbb{1}[a^{(i)} = a]$.
The intuition is that complex problems admit many correct paths but typically only one correct final answer, so consistency across paths is a strong (if uncalibrated) signal of correctness.
Empirically, with $N = 40$ samples and PaLM-540B, GSM8K jumps from 56.5% (greedy CoT) to 74.4% (+17.9 absolute), and consistent gains appear on every arithmetic and commonsense benchmark tested.
The paper also defines a *weighted* variant that uses model log-probability of each path as a soft weight, but reports that uniform-weight majority vote is essentially as good — a finding echoed throughout the BoN literature and one that supports CoT-CP's plan to treat vote-share as a stand-alone, model-free score.

## Method
- **What is computed at each step**: Nothing per-step. The aggregation operates on terminal answers $a^{(i)}$ extracted from each sampled trace.
- **Training data**: None — self-consistency is *training-free*, applied at decoding time on top of any CoT-prompted base model.
- **Score function definition** (the canonical formula):
  $$\hat{a}_{\text{SC}} = \arg\max_{a} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \mathbb{1}\!\left[ a^{(i)} = a \right] \qquad \text{where } (r^{(i)}, a^{(i)}) \sim p_\theta(\cdot \mid \text{prompt})$$
  Equivalently, marginalize $p(a \mid \text{prompt}) = \sum_{r} p(a, r \mid \text{prompt})$ via Monte Carlo with the model as the sampler.
  Weighted variant: $\hat{a} = \arg\max_a \sum_i w^{(i)} \mathbb{1}[a^{(i)} = a]$ with $w^{(i)} \propto \exp(\frac{1}{|r^{(i)}|}\log p_\theta(r^{(i)}))$ (length-normalized log-prob).
- **Aggregation to final**: Pure majority vote at the final-answer level.

## Reported results
- **Setup**: $N = 40$ samples, temperature $T = 0.7$ (PaLM, LaMDA), top-$k$/$p$ sampling for the others.
- **Base models**: UL2-20B, GPT-3 (code-davinci-001/002), LaMDA-137B, PaLM-540B.
- **PaLM-540B headline gains**:

  | Benchmark | Greedy CoT | Self-Consistency | Δ |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | GSM8K | 56.5 | 74.4 | **+17.9** |
  | SVAMP | 79.0 | 86.6 | +7.6 |
  | AQuA | 35.8 | 48.3 | +12.5 |
  | StrategyQA | 75.3 | 81.6 | +6.3 |
  | ARC-challenge | — | — | +3.9 |

- **Other benchmarks with consistent gains**: MultiArith, ASDiv, AddSub, CommonsenseQA, ARC-easy.
- **Comparison baselines**: outperforms beam-search, sample-and-rank, and prompt-ensemble under matched compute.
- **Robustness**: holds across model families (UL2-20B → PaLM-540B), prompt formats, and sampling strategies.
- **N-scaling**: gain grows with $N$ up to ~40 then plateaus.
- **Weighted variant**: log-prob-length-normalized vote weights $w^{(i)} \propto \exp(\frac{1}{|r^{(i)}|}\log p_\theta(r^{(i)}))$; gain is essentially the same as uniform vote — log-prob is uninformative once a single chain is sampled to completion.

## How CoT-CP uses this paper
- **Role**: **Candidate step-level score function** + headline test-time-scaling baseline.
- **Specific use**:
  - At each step $t$, define **vote-share-at-step-$t$**: $s_t = $ fraction of the $N$ self-consistency continuations from prefix $s_{1:t}$ that agree with the modal completion. This is one of the simplest candidate non-conformity scores in CoT-CP and is plug-compatible with PRM-based scores.
  - SC's terminal majority vote is the trivial answer-level aggregator — the **baseline** that CoT-CP must beat in compute-vs-accuracy plots ("Self-Consistency monkey baseline").
  - Use the +17.9 GSM8K number as the canonical "test-time scaling works" reference in the introduction.
  - We do **not** use the weighted log-prob variant — log-prob calibration is poor on long CoT (cf. Reasoning Models Better Express Their Confidence, D2).
- **What CoT-CP adds on top**:
  - SC has *no* statistical guarantee — its accuracy curves are empirical. CoT-CP delivers $P(\hat{a} \text{ correct}) \geq 1-\alpha$ on any new prompt under exchangeability.
  - SC uses a *fixed* $N$. CoT-CP allocates samples *adaptively where needed* (high $s_t$ uncertainty), spending more compute on hard prefixes and less on easy ones — provably more efficient than uniform $N$ in the regime where most problems are easy and a few are hard (the GSM8K and MATH-500 regime).
  - Sister adaptive-stopping methods (Adaptive-Consistency C3, ESC C4) get partway there but remain heuristic and prompt-level; CoT-CP is step-level and frequentist.
- **Required citation**: §2 (sample-and-marginalize formulation), §3 (formal majority-vote definition + weighted variant), §4.2 (GSM8K headline numbers), §5 (full benchmark suite).

## Limitations (what this paper does *not* deliver)
- Fixed $N$ — no adaptive allocation; SC's compute is wasted on easy problems.
- Requires a verifiable-answer extraction step (regex / structured output) — only works in fixed-answer domains, not free-form.
- No statistical guarantee; the "+17.9 GSM8K" is empirical accuracy.
- Vote-share / agreement is not a calibrated probability of correctness; can be overconfident on hard problems where many traces share the same wrong answer.
- Per-step vote-share is not directly defined in the paper — extending SC to step-level requires sampling continuations from each prefix (cost $\mathcal{O}(N \cdot T)$).

## Open questions for CoT-CP design
1. Vote-share-at-step-$t$ is well-defined only for prefixes from which we sample multiple continuations. Cost = $N$ continuations per step. CoT-CP would only invoke this score at uncertain steps (where a cheaper score is borderline) — adaptive *score escalation*.
2. SC's $N=40$ is a hyperparameter, not a calibrated quantity. CoT-CP can recover an *adaptive* $N$ via CRC: stop sampling when the empirical vote-share entropy crosses a calibrated threshold $\hat{q}$.
3. Length-normalized log-prob (the weighted-SC variant) is roughly proportional to "model believes its own trace". Reasoning models (Yoon+ 2025, §D2) are better calibrated than instruction-tuned models — does the CoT-CP gain shrink on reasoning models? Important ablation.
