## Abstract (verbatim)

Large Language Models (LLMs) have shown great potential in reasoning tasks
through test-time scaling methods like self-consistency with majority voting.
However, this approach often leads to diminishing returns in accuracy and high
computational overhead. To address these challenges, we introduce Deep Think
with Confidence (DeepConf), a simple yet powerful method that enhances both
reasoning efficiency and performance at test time. DeepConf leverages
model-internal confidence signals to dynamically filter out low-quality
reasoning traces during or after generation. It requires no additional model
training or hyperparameter tuning and can be seamlessly integrated into
existing serving frameworks. We evaluate DeepConf across a variety of
reasoning tasks and the latest open-source models, including Qwen 3 and
GPT-OSS series. Notably, on challenging benchmarks such as AIME 2025,
DeepConf@512 achieves up to 99.9% accuracy and reduces generated tokens by up
to 84.7% compared to full parallel thinking.

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

DeepConf is the closest paper to CoT-CP: it uses **token-level confidence**
(softmax probability of the chosen token) **averaged over a span** ("group
confidence") as the conformity signal that decides whether a sampled CoT trace
is kept or dropped during/after self-consistency sampling. The mechanism has
two flavors: an **offline** filter that, after N traces are sampled, drops the
lowest-confidence groups before majority voting; and an **online** mode that
terminates a trace early when its running group-confidence falls below a
data-dependent threshold derived from a small warm-up batch. The headline
empirical result — AIME-25 99.9% accuracy at -84.7% tokens with Qwen3 / GPT-OSS
families — directly invades CoT-CP's target benchmarks. The framing
"requires...no hyperparameter tuning" is true only in the trivial sense that
the threshold is computed automatically from the warm-up traces; that
threshold is **still uncalibrated and offers no coverage guarantee**, which is
exactly the gap CoT-CP must claim.

## Method

- **Step-level signal used**: Token-level confidence c_i = -log p(token_i)
  (or equivalently the softmax probability of the chosen token), aggregated
  into a **group confidence** by averaging over a sliding window (the paper
  refers to it as "group" or "span" confidence). Variants reported include
  bottom-10% group confidence, mean group confidence, and tail confidence (the
  last K tokens before the answer).
- **Stopping/branching rule**: Two regimes.
  1. *Offline filtering*: sample N traces (e.g. N=512), compute each trace's
     group-confidence summary, drop the bottom-η% by confidence, take majority
     vote on the survivors.
  2. *Online early-stop*: during generation, if the running group-confidence
     of a trace falls below a threshold s* it is terminated; freed budget is
     redirected to new traces.
- **Threshold tuning**: The threshold s* is derived from a **warm-up set** of
  N_init traces (default N_init = 16): compute the group-confidence
  distribution of those warm-up traces, and set s* to the η-th percentile
  (paper uses configurations like η=10% or η=90% depending on aggressive vs.
  conservative filtering). There is **no held-out calibration set with a
  coverage target**; s* is purely the empirical η-quantile of the warm-up
  batch on the same prompt.
- **Per-prompt vs per-step adaptation**: **Per-prompt for the threshold**
  (warm-up traces are prompt-specific) and **per-trace for the stop decision**
  (each new trace is gated against s*). It is *not* a step-level guarantee;
  it is a trace-level filter.

## Reported empirical results (the headline numbers)

- **AIME 2025 / Qwen3-8B**: 99.9% accuracy, -84.7% tokens vs. Cons@512 (the
  flagship number in the abstract).
- **AIME 2025 / GPT-OSS-120B**: ~99% accuracy with similar token savings.
- **AIME 2024**: comparable >97% with similar reductions.
- **HMMT 2025, BRUMO 2025**: similar pattern — near-saturation accuracy at
  large token reduction.
- **GPQA-Diamond, MATH-500**: smaller but consistent gains; GPQA in
  mid-70s for the strongest backbones.
- All numbers are at compute budget N=512 sampled CoT traces; "DeepConf@512"
  is the headline configuration.

## Where their threshold is heuristic (positioning)

- The threshold s* is the **empirical η-percentile** of group-confidences
  from N_init warm-up traces on the same prompt. This is a sample quantile,
  not a finite-sample-valid quantile in the conformal-prediction sense — it
  carries no coverage guarantee.
- The choice of percentile η (e.g. 10% vs 90%) is a **knob**: the paper
  reports curves over η and picks per-task. That is exactly the "tuned
  per-benchmark" pattern the abstract claims to avoid. The "no
  hyperparameter tuning" claim is restricted to *automatic* computation of
  s* from warm-up; the *family* of thresholds (which percentile, which
  aggregation, which window size) is empirically chosen.
- The aggregation choice — bottom-10% vs mean vs tail — is also empirical;
  the paper sweeps and reports the best-performing aggregator per benchmark.
  None of this generalizes to a guarantee on a new prompt distribution.
- If you change benchmark (move from AIME to a calculus or open-domain
  reasoning task with different trace-length distribution) or model (Qwen3 →
  GPT-OSS-120B), the warm-up distribution shifts, the η-percentile shifts,
  and the implicit accuracy guarantee silently degrades. There is no
  finite-sample, distribution-free statement protecting accuracy under
  distribution drift.

## How CoT-CP positions against this

- **Empirical overlap**: Massive — same benchmarks (AIME-24/25, MATH-500,
  GPQA-Diamond, HMMT) and same family of base models (Qwen3, GPT-OSS,
  DeepSeek-R1 derivatives). DeepConf is the must-beat baseline.
- **What CoT-CP adds**:
  - Distribution-free coverage statement P(final answer correct) ≥ 1 − α
    via Conformal Risk Control (Angelopoulos+ 2022), valid under
    exchangeability with the calibration set.
  - Calibration on **PRM800K** step-labels (or equivalent) supplies a
    *transferable* quantile q̂ that does not need a per-prompt warm-up.
  - **Step-level** instrumentation: CoT-CP gates at the step boundary, not
    the trace boundary, so it inherits DeepConf-style token savings *plus*
    propagates a step-level signal into a sequence-level guarantee.
  - DeepConf's group-confidence is a perfectly valid *score function* for
    CoT-CP — CoT-CP is the calibration shell that wraps DeepConf-style
    scores.
- **Required experimental baseline**: yes — must reproduce DeepConf@512 on
  AIME-25 / AIME-24 / MATH-500 / GPQA-Diamond with Qwen3-8B and GPT-OSS-120B
  (or contemporaneous frontier model). Anything less and the reviewers will
  call it.
- **Required citation**: §1 (Introduction — flagship related work), §3
  (Related Work — span-confidence-as-score subsection), §5 (Experiments —
  baseline column), §6 (Discussion — DeepConf as a special case of
  uncalibrated CoT-CP).
