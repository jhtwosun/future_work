## Abstract (verbatim)

"LLMs can solve complex tasks by generating long, multi-step reasoning chains. Test-time scaling (TTS) can further improve performance by sampling multiple variants of intermediate reasoning steps, verifying their correctness, and selecting the best steps for continuation. However, existing verification approaches, such as Process Reward Models (PRMs), are computationally expensive and require large-scale human or model-generated annotations. We propose a lightweight alternative for step-level reasoning verification based on probing the internal states of LLMs. We train a transformer-based probe that uses the internal states of a frozen LLM to estimate the credibility of its reasoning steps during generation. Annotation can be provided either by a larger LLM (e.g., DeepSeek-R1) or in a self-supervised manner by the original model itself. The probes are lightweight, containing fewer than 10M parameters. Across multiple domains, including mathematics, planning, and general knowledge question answering, our probes match or exceed the performance of PRMs that are up to 810x larger. These results suggest that LLM internal states encode confidence in their reasoning processes and can serve as reliable signals for step verification, offering a promising path toward scalable, generalizable TTS and more introspective LLMs."

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

Train a tiny transformer probe (the "uncertainty head", UHead) on the **frozen** internal hidden states of an LLM to predict, at each reasoning step, whether that step is correct. The probe is small enough (<10M params) that it adds negligible inference cost compared to running a full PRM. Training labels do not require human annotation — a strong external model (DeepSeek-R1) or the base model itself (self-supervised) auto-labels intermediate steps. Despite being up to 810× smaller than competing PRMs, the probes match or beat them on math, planning, and general-knowledge reasoning. The probe outputs serve as the per-step "credibility" signal driving best-of-N / beam-style step selection.

## Method
- **Step-level signal used**: Hidden-state activations from a frozen LLM (the paper indicates final-layer hidden states feed the probe; specific layer choice ablated in paper). Output is a scalar credibility score per reasoning step.
- **Trigger threshold**: No fixed threshold — the score is used as a ranking signal in TTS (beam search / best-of-N). When discretized, threshold is empirical, tuned on dev set.
- **Action triggered**: Step selection / pruning during multi-step sampling — keep top-scoring continuations, drop low-scoring ones.
- **Training requirement**: Train a small transformer probe (<10M params). Frozen base LLM is **not** fine-tuned. Two label sources: (a) **Distillation labels** — use DeepSeek-R1 as a teacher to judge step correctness; (b) **Self-supervised labels** — labels derived from the base model's own rollouts (e.g., outcome-supervision propagated to steps via Math-Shepherd-style sampling).

## Reported results
- Probes have **<10M parameters** vs. PRMs **up to 810× larger** (so ~7B-class PRMs).
- Probes **match or exceed** PRM performance across **mathematics, planning, and general-knowledge QA** domains.
- Specific dataset numbers (GSM8K, MATH, ProcessBench, etc.) are in the full paper tables — abstract reports the headline 810× compression ratio at parity.
- Self-supervised labelling shown competitive with R1-distilled labels — no external strong model strictly required.

## Why this is HIGH threat
- **Direct quote on niche overlap:** "We propose a lightweight alternative for step-level reasoning verification based on probing the internal states of LLMs ... fewer than 10M parameters ... match or exceed the performance of PRMs that are up to 810x larger."
- This paper occupies exactly the "cheap step-level UQ for TTS" slot CoT-CP wants to occupy. It even uses internal states (a richer signal than logit entropy), so any pure-entropy CoT-CP variant looks weaker.
- Auto-labeling pipeline removes the "human annotation cost" objection that older PRM-based step verifiers had — this is the same objection CoT-CP would lean on.

## What CoT-CP adds vs this paper
- **Calibration layer that gives P(correct) >= 1-alpha via CRC** — UHead emits an *uncalibrated* credibility score; CoT-CP wraps **any** such score (including UHead) with a conformal quantile threshold, turning a heuristic ranking into a sequence-level error guarantee.
- UHead does not provide per-prompt risk control: a low UHead score means "less credible" but not "exceeds budget alpha". CoT-CP makes that translation rigorous.
- UHead is a **selection** mechanism (TTS); CoT-CP can additionally drive **adaptive compute** (skip verifier on high-confidence steps, invoke heavier verifier on low-confidence steps) with statistical guarantees.
- UHead requires probe training data; CoT-CP only needs a held-out calibration set (orders of magnitude smaller).
- **Required experimental baseline**: **YES** — must compare CoT-CP-with-UHead-as-score against UHead-with-empirical-threshold on at least GSM8K + MATH + ProcessBench. Showing CoT-CP delivers the same or better accuracy *with calibrated coverage* over UHead's uncalibrated ranking is the core empirical story.
