## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

Speculative *reasoning* (not just speculative decoding): a small "draft" model generates a candidate step in the CoT; a larger "target" model would normally verify each draft step, which is expensive. ConfSpec exploits an asymmetry: small draft models are claimed to be **well-calibrated** within their competence range, so a high draft-confidence on a step is a reliable signal that the step is correct. When draft confidence exceeds a threshold, the step is **accepted without target verification**, skipping the expensive call. Low-confidence draft steps fall back to target-model verification or regeneration. End-to-end this gives up to 2.24× speedup at target-model accuracy parity.

## Method
- **Step-level signal used**: **Draft model's own confidence** on the generated step (likely a token-confidence aggregate like average log-prob or step-level entropy proxy on the draft, evaluated *step-of-CoT* not token-of-vocab).
- **Trigger threshold**: Confidence threshold tau on the draft's step confidence. Above tau: accept the speculated step. Below tau: defer to the larger target model. Threshold is empirical / tuned, not calibrated to a sequence-level error budget.
- **Action triggered**: (a) Accept draft step (skip target verification — the speedup case). (b) Reject / defer to target (the safety case).
- **Training requirement**: **None** beyond having a small draft model + a larger target model. No external judge / reward model required.
