## Abstract (verbatim)

"Multi-step processes via large language models (LLMs) have proven effective for solving complex reasoning tasks. However, the depth of exploration of the reasoning procedure can significantly affect the task performance. Existing methods to automatically decide the depth often lead to high cost and a lack of flexibility. To address these issues, we propose Entropy-based Exploration Depth Conduction (Entro-duction), a novel method that dynamically adjusts the exploration depth during multi-step reasoning by monitoring LLM's output entropy and variance entropy. We employ these two features to capture the model's uncertainty of the current step and the fluctuation of uncertainty across consecutive reasoning steps. Based on the observed entropy changes, the LLM selects whether to deepen, expand, or stop exploration according to the probability, which facilitates the trade-off between the reasoning accuracy and exploration effectiveness. Experimental results across four benchmark datasets demonstrate the efficacy of Entro-duction."

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

At each reasoning step, monitor (a) **output entropy** H_t — the model's instantaneous uncertainty over its next token / step — and (b) **variance entropy** Var_t — how much that uncertainty has been fluctuating across recent consecutive steps. These two scalar features summarize "is the model confident here?" and "is the model becoming more or less confident over time?". The method then **probabilistically** decides one of three actions: *deepen* (keep going on the current chain), *expand* (branch out to alternative continuations), or *stop* (commit / exit). The motivation is to spend more compute only where the model is uncertain *and* uncertainty is volatile, otherwise stop early.

## Method
- **Step-level signal used**: Two scalars per step:
  - Output entropy H_t = -sum_v p(v | context) log p(v | context) — Shannon entropy of next-token distribution at step t (token-level, aggregated over the step's tokens).
  - Variance entropy Var_t = variance of {H_{t-W+1}, ..., H_t} over a sliding window W of consecutive reasoning steps (window size empirical; not the focus of headline ablation).
- **Trigger threshold**: Entropy values mapped through a learned/tuned probability function that picks deepen / expand / stop. Thresholds are **dataset-tuned** rather than calibrated — high H_t and high Var_t push toward *expand* (branch), low H_t pushes toward *stop*, mid-range pushes toward *deepen*.
- **Action triggered**: One of {deepen, expand, stop}. Branching ("expand") is the costly action, used selectively when both uncertainty and uncertainty-volatility are high.
- **Training requirement**: **None** for the underlying LLM. Light hyperparameter tuning of the threshold/probability function on a dev split per dataset.

## Reported results
- Evaluated on **four benchmarks** including math (GSM8K, MATH) and commonsense reasoning (CommonsenseQA) plus one further reasoning benchmark.
- Reports accuracy improvements over fixed-depth CoT and fixed-branching ToT baselines while reducing exploration cost — exact deltas are in the paper's main results table; abstract claims "efficacy" across all four.
- Output-entropy + variance-entropy combination dominates either signal alone in their ablations.

## Why this is HIGH threat
- **Direct quote on niche overlap:** "Entro-duction ... dynamically adjusts the exploration depth during multi-step reasoning by monitoring LLM's output entropy and variance entropy ... selects whether to deepen, expand, or stop exploration."
- This is *exactly* the "use a per-step uncertainty signal to decide branch vs. continue vs. exit" frame CoT-CP wants. The two-feature design (instantaneous entropy + volatility) is more sophisticated than naive single-threshold gating, which makes head-to-head comparison non-trivial.
- The action set {deepen, expand, stop} maps 1:1 onto CoT-CP's intended action menu (continue / branch / exit).

## What CoT-CP adds vs this paper
- **Calibration layer that gives P(correct) >= 1-alpha via CRC** — Entro-duction's thresholds are tuned per dataset and provide **no risk guarantee**. Move the same prompt distribution into a slightly shifted domain and the thresholds may silently miscalibrate.
- Entro-duction's signal is purely **logit-distribution entropy**; CoT-CP can wrap richer signals (UHead, PRM, semantic entropy) and still deliver the guarantee.
- Variance-entropy idea is genuinely useful and worth borrowing as one component of CoT-CP's per-step score s_t — but CoT-CP would then submit s_t to CRC for a calibrated quantile, not to a hand-tuned probability table.
- **Required experimental baseline**: **YES** — Entro-duction is a prime entropy-only baseline. Run on GSM8K + MATH with the same base model; compare (i) accuracy at matched compute, (ii) coverage of correct-step set at target alpha (Entro-duction has no notion of this — that's the point).
- **Required citation**: §3 (Related Work, "entropy-driven step gating"), §5 (Experiments, baseline). Also discuss in §4 ablation: "Variance entropy as an s_t component."
