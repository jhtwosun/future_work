## Abstract (verbatim)

"Autoregressive pre-trained models combined with decoding methods have achieved impressive performance on complex reasoning tasks. While mainstream decoding strategies such as beam search can generate plausible candidate sets, they often lack provable coverage guarantees, and struggle to effectively balance search efficiency with the need for versatile trajectories, particularly those involving long-tail sequences that are essential in certain real-world applications. To address these limitations, we propose CoVeR, a novel model-free decoding strategy within the conformal prediction framework that simultaneously maintains a compact search space and ensures high coverage probability over desirable trajectories. Theoretically, we establish a PAC-style generalization bound, guaranteeing that CoVeR asymptotically achieves a coverage rate of at least 1 - alpha for any target level alpha in (0,1)."

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

CoVeR re-frames decoding as a conformal-prediction problem: at each generation step it constructs a *prediction set of next-token continuations* whose joint coverage of "desirable trajectories" is provably at least 1 - alpha. Tokens that share a step-prefix are grouped (clustered) by the empirical distribution of their nonconformity scores, so calibration happens over **token-clusters at step boundaries** rather than over whole sequences or single tokens. The procedure is model-free (treats the LM as a black-box scorer) and yields a PAC-style generalization bound. The headline contribution is a compact-yet-versatile candidate set that retains long-tail trajectories that beam search drops.

## Method

- **Conformity score**: an autoregressive next-token / step-prefix score, used to form *empirical score distributions* over tokens that share a generation prefix. Clusters of tokens with similar score distributions are calibrated jointly.
- **Calibration set**: held-out trajectories from the same generation distribution; calibration produces a quantile threshold over the empirical (per-cluster) score distribution.
- **Coverage statement**: PAC-style asymptotic bound — coverage of "desirable trajectories" is at least 1 - alpha for any alpha in (0,1).
- **Granularity**: **token-cluster at the step / prefix level** — the decoding step is the calibration unit, but the unit is defined by *token clusters* (shared prefix + similar score distribution), not by *semantic reasoning steps*.

## How CoT-CP positions against this

- **Overlap with CoT-CP**: This is the closest published competitor to CoT-CP. Both calibrate at *step granularity* during autoregressive generation and both promise (1 - alpha) coverage. CoVeR is **the** paper a reviewer will name as prior art for "step-level CP for LLM reasoning."
- **What CoT-CP adds**:
  1. **Semantic-step granularity, not token-cluster granularity.** CoVeR's calibration unit is a *token cluster sharing a prefix*; CoT-CP's unit is a **semantic reasoning step** (sentence / proof step / equation / sub-claim, in the spirit of Mohri-Hashimoto Conformal Factuality). Token-cluster boundaries are syntactic; CoT-CP's are reasoning-graph-aligned, which matters for selective-accuracy guarantees on the *answer correctness*, not on next-token plausibility.
  2. **Selective accuracy on the final answer**, not coverage of "desirable trajectories." CoT-CP's coverage statement is about whether the *final reasoning answer* is correct given the chain passed step-level abstention thresholds — a substantively different guarantee.
  3. **Compatibility with closed-source / API LLMs.** CoVeR is model-free in spirit but its score-clustering is naturally tied to logits over a known vocabulary; CoT-CP's semantic-step decomposition can be driven by an external verifier that only sees text, so it works on API-only LLMs.
  4. **Per-step abstention / human-in-the-loop branch points.** CoT-CP exposes an abstain-or-defer decision at each semantic step; CoVeR is a pure decoding-set construction.
- **Required experimental baseline**: We MUST run CoVeR on the same CoT benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH, StrategyQA, ProofWriter) at matched alpha and report set size and answer-level selective accuracy. If we cannot beat CoVeR on selective accuracy at matched coverage, the contribution collapses to a granularity-positioning argument and the threat is severe.
- **Required citation**: yes, **section 2 (related work) and again at the methods section** where step granularity is defined. We must explicitly contrast token-cluster vs. semantic-step.
