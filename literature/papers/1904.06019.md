## Abstract (verbatim)
We extend conformal prediction methodology beyond the case of exchangeable data. In particular, we show that a weighted version of conformal prediction can be used to compute distribution-free prediction intervals for problems in which the test and training covariate distributions differ, but the likelihood ratio between these two distributions is known---or, in practice, can be estimated accurately with access to a large set of unlabeled data (test covariate points). Our weighted extension of conformal prediction also applies more generally, to settings in which the data satisfies a certain weighted notion of exchangeability. We discuss other potential applications of our new conformal methodology, including latent variable and missing data problems.

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)
Standard conformal prediction is valid only under exchangeability; covariate shift P_train(X) != P_test(X) breaks this. The paper introduces *weighted conformal prediction*: the empirical quantile over calibration scores is replaced by a *likelihood-ratio-weighted* quantile w(X_i) = dP_test(X_i) / dP_train(X_i), and the resulting prediction interval again attains 1 - alpha marginal coverage. The construction is exact when the likelihood ratio is known and is the foundation for any setting where calibration and deployment populations differ. The paper formalizes a strictly weaker condition the authors call *weighted exchangeability*, generalizing classical exchangeability and covering covariate-shift, latent-variable, and missing-data scenarios with a unified proof.

## Method / theorem statements
- **Weighted exchangeability**: random variables Z_1, ..., Z_{n+1} are weighted exchangeable with weights w_1, ..., w_{n+1} if their joint density factorizes into a symmetric core times prod_i w_i(z_i). Covariate shift with P_test = w * P_train satisfies this with w_i = w(X_i).
- **Weighted CP construction**: form normalized weights p_i^w(x) = w(X_i) / (sum_{j=1}^n w(X_j) + w(x)) for i = 1..n, and p_{n+1}^w(x) = w(x) / (sum_{j=1}^n w(X_j) + w(x)). Define C(x) = { y : s(x, y) <= Quantile_{1-alpha} ( sum_{i=1}^n p_i^w(x) * delta_{s(X_i, Y_i)} + p_{n+1}^w(x) * delta_{+infty} ) }.
- **Theorem 1 (weighted CP coverage, the result we cite as Theorem 3 backbone)**: under weighted exchangeability with weights w, P(Y_{n+1} in C(X_{n+1})) >= 1 - alpha, with no distributional or model assumptions beyond knowledge of w.
- **Assumptions**: weighted exchangeability holds (covariate shift with known/estimated likelihood ratio is the canonical example); the likelihood ratio is bounded almost surely or otherwise integrable.
- **Slack terms**: same O(1/n) discreteness slack as unweighted CP, plus an additional gap that scales with effective sample size n_eff = (sum w_i)^2 / sum w_i^2 (small effective sample under heavy weight tails).
- **Estimation note**: when w is unknown but a large pool of unlabeled test covariates is available, w can be estimated via density-ratio estimation (e.g., a probabilistic classifier) and the guarantee degrades smoothly with the estimation error.

## Reported results
- Synthetic regression under covariate shift: standard CP undercovers (e.g., 80% empirical at alpha = 0.1) while weighted CP achieves nominal 90% coverage (see paper for details).
- Airfoil-self-noise / wine-quality benchmarks: weighted CP recovers nominal coverage under simulated shifts where unweighted CP fails (see paper for tables).
