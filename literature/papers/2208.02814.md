## Abstract (verbatim)
We extend conformal prediction to control the expected value of any monotone loss function. The algorithm generalizes split conformal prediction together with its coverage guarantee. Like conformal prediction, the conformal risk control procedure is tight up to an $\mathcal{O}(1/n)$ factor. We also introduce extensions of the idea to distribution shift, quantile risk control, multiple and adversarial risk control, and expectations of U-statistics. Worked examples from computer vision and natural language processing demonstrate the usage of our algorithm to bound the false negative rate, graph distance, and token-level F1-score.

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)
Conformal Risk Control (CRC) generalizes split CP from the special case of 0-1 miscoverage loss to *any* loss L_lambda(X, Y) that is monotone non-increasing in a scalar threshold lambda and bounded above by some B. Choose lambda_hat as the smallest value of lambda such that an empirical-mean-plus-correction surrogate is below the target risk alpha; then E[L_{lambda_hat}(X_test, Y_test)] <= alpha holds in finite samples without distributional assumptions. The cost is the same O(1/n) slack as standard CP, and the algorithm reduces to classical split CP exactly when L is the indicator 1{Y not in C_lambda(X)}. CRC is the *backbone machinery* for many downstream LLM/CV calibration applications because most useful losses (FNR, graph distance, token F1, hallucination-rate) are monotone in a tunable set-size or threshold knob.

## Method / theorem statements
- **Setup**: loss L_lambda : X x Y -> (-infty, B], monotone non-increasing in lambda, with lim_{lambda -> lambda_max} L_lambda <= alpha almost surely. Calibration losses L_i(lambda) = L_lambda(X_i, Y_i), i = 1..n.
- **CRC threshold rule**: lambda_hat = inf { lambda : (n/(n+1)) * (1/n) * sum_{i=1}^n L_i(lambda) + B/(n+1) <= alpha }.
- **Theorem 1 (CRC marginal risk bound, the result we cite)**: under exchangeability of (X_i, Y_i)_{i=1..n+1}, E[L_{lambda_hat}(X_{n+1}, Y_{n+1})] <= alpha. Tight up to O(1/n).
- **Reduction**: setting L_lambda = 1{Y not in C_lambda(X)} recovers split-CP marginal coverage exactly.
- **Assumptions**: exchangeability; loss bounded by B; monotonicity in lambda; right-continuity of expected loss in lambda.
- **Slack terms**: discrete quantile correction (n+1) and bound term B/(n+1); both O(1/n).
- **Extensions sketched** (each formal): distribution-shift CRC via likelihood-ratio reweighting (compatible with Tibshirani et al. 2019); quantile risk control; multiple / adversarial risk control; CRC for U-statistics.

## Reported results
- **CV (polyp segmentation)**: CRC bounds false negative rate at chosen alpha; achieves the prescribed FNR with empirical risk near alpha (see paper for numerical tables).
- **NLP (open-domain QA / token F1)**: CRC controls 1 - F1 in expectation across reported splits (see paper for details).
- **Graph distance**: bounds expected distance between predicted and true target in hierarchical labels (see paper for details).
- All experiments verify the predicted O(1/n) slack and validity under the standard splits used.
