## Abstract (verbatim)

A popular approach for improving the correctness of output from large
language models (LLMs) is Self-Consistency - poll the LLM multiple times and
output the most frequent solution. Existing Self-Consistency techniques
always generate a constant number of samples per question, where a better
approach will be to non-uniformly distribute the available budget based on
the amount of agreement in the samples generated so far. In response, we
introduce Adaptive-Consistency, a cost-efficient, model-agnostic technique
that dynamically adjusts the number of samples per question using a
lightweight stopping criterion. Our experiments over 17 reasoning and code
generation datasets and three LLMs demonstrate that Adaptive-Consistency
reduces sample budget by up to 7.9 times with an average accuracy drop of
less than 0.1%.

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

Adaptive-Consistency replaces the fixed-N self-consistency budget with a
**Bayesian stopping rule** on the running tally of sampled answers. Treat
the answer-vote counts as observations from a Dirichlet–Multinomial (or
Beta-Binomial in the binary majority-vs-rest collapse), maintain a posterior
over which answer is the population majority, and **stop sampling once that
posterior probability exceeds a threshold C_thresh** (the public release
exposes this as `stop_criteria='beta'`). The paper reports up to 7.9×
sample-budget reduction across 17 reasoning/coding datasets at <0.1%
accuracy drop on three LLMs. The crucial tell for our positioning: C_thresh
is a hand-set Bayesian credibility level (typically ~0.95) and the
Dirichlet/Beta prior is a fixed hyperparameter — no frequentist coverage
statement, no held-out calibration, no model- or distribution-aware
adaptation.

## Method

- **Step-level signal used**: Vote-share over the sampled answers so far —
  i.e. the empirical multinomial counts (a_1: c_1, a_2: c_2, ...) after
  m samples. This is a **prompt-level** signal (not step-level): it requires
  finishing each CoT to read off its final answer.
- **Stopping/branching rule**: After each new sample, update a posterior
  over the answer distribution. The default `beta` criterion collapses to
  the two-class Beta-Binomial: posterior on p_majority is Beta(c_1+α,
  m−c_1+β), and the rule is **stop if P(p_majority is the population
  majority | counts) ≥ C_thresh**. The paper also reports Dirichlet, entropy,
  and majority-margin variants; Beta is the default.
- **Threshold tuning**: C_thresh is set as a Bayesian credibility level
  (default ~0.95), not via held-out calibration. The paper sweeps C_thresh
  on the cost-vs-accuracy frontier; users pick a point. The Beta prior
  α=β=1 (uniform) is a fixed hyperparameter.
- **Per-prompt vs per-step adaptation**: **Per-prompt only**. The number of
  samples is adapted per question, but each sample is a complete CoT trace —
  no step-level instrumentation, no early termination *within* a trace.

## Reported empirical results (the headline numbers)

- **Models**: three LLMs spanning Vicuna-13B, Code-Davinci-002 / GPT-3.5,
  and GPT-4-class (the sample-step-by-step.info site lists "two LLMs" plus
  Vicuna; arXiv abstract says "three LLMs").
- **Benchmarks (17 total)**: GSM8K, SVAMP, ASDiv, AQuA, MATH (subset),
  MultiArith, AddSub, SingleEq, CommonsenseQA, StrategyQA, Date, Sports,
  Coin Flip, Last Letters, Object Counting, MBPP, HumanEval (or close
  equivalents — the paper groups them into Mathematical / Logical / Other
  Reasoning + Code).
- **Headline**: up to **7.9× sample reduction**, average accuracy drop
  <0.1%. On GSM8K with GPT-3.5, ~3-4× reduction at neutral accuracy.
- The Beta criterion is the empirical winner across stopping-rule variants.

## Where their threshold is heuristic (positioning)

- C_thresh is a **Bayesian credibility level** (e.g. 0.95). It is
  *not* a frequentist coverage guarantee on final-answer correctness — the
  posterior is over "which answer is the population majority", not over
  "the majority answer is correct." Even if P(majority answer = population
  majority) = 0.95, the population-majority answer can itself be wrong, so
  the *correctness* coverage is uncalibrated and dataset-dependent.
- The Beta prior α=β=1 is a **fixed hyperparameter** with no
  data-driven justification; the prior choice silently shifts behavior on
  rare-answer-distribution prompts (e.g. AIME, where the answer is a
  3-digit integer with high entropy).
- The "lightweight" framing in the abstract masks that C_thresh must be
  picked by the user and that the paper sweeps it on the eval set itself.
  No held-out calibration set is used to convert C_thresh into a
  finite-sample correctness guarantee.
- Brittle to **answer-space cardinality**: on multiple-choice benchmarks
  (GPQA, CommonsenseQA) the Beta-Binomial collapse is reasonable; on
  open-ended math (AIME, MATH) the Dirichlet prior over a large answer
  alphabet produces overconfident posteriors after few samples — a known
  failure mode.
- If you change benchmark answer-distribution or model agreement-rate
  curves, C_thresh ↔ accuracy mapping shifts and there is no statistical
  guarantee.

## How CoT-CP positions against this

- **Empirical overlap**: Partial — overlapping on GSM8K and MATH; AIME and
  GPQA-Diamond are not in the original 17 datasets but are the natural
  modern extension we will run for both methods.
- **What CoT-CP adds**:
  - Distribution-free coverage statement P(correct) ≥ 1 − α via CRC —
    a *frequentist* finite-sample guarantee on final-answer correctness,
    not on "majority-answer-is-population-majority".
  - Explicit calibration on PRM800K-style step labels rather than a fixed
    Beta(1,1) prior.
  - **Step-level** adaptation: CoT-CP can stop *inside* a trace, where
    Adaptive-Consistency must finish each trace before reading its
    answer-vote.
  - Removes the need for a per-benchmark C_thresh sweep — the user picks
    α, the calibration set picks q̂.
  - Vote-share itself is a valid score function for CoT-CP — CoT-CP
    subsumes Adaptive-Consistency as the special case "score = vote-share,
    aggregation = trace-level".
- **Required experimental baseline**: yes — reproduce Adaptive-Consistency
  with the Beta criterion on MATH-500, GSM8K, AIME-2024/25, GPQA-Diamond
  with at least one shared model (Qwen3-8B or DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B)
  and report the cost-vs-accuracy frontier head-to-head.
- **Required citation**: §1 (Introduction — closest non-CP analog), §3
  (Related Work — adaptive-sampling-via-stopping subsection), §5
  (Experiments — baseline row on each benchmark), §6 (Discussion —
  Adaptive-Consistency as a Bayesian-prior special case of CoT-CP).
