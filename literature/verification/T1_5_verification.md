### Closest-five deep reads

**1. CoVe (Dhuliawala+ ACL Findings 2024, 2309.11495).** Prompt the LLM to (i) draft, (ii) generate verification *questions*, (iii) answer them independently, (iv) revise. The verification questions are *generated by the model* — they are not a fixed taxonomic checklist. Variants Joint / 2-Step / Factored / Factor+Revise. Already in our 30-paper §E4 envelope.

**2. PedCoT (2405.06705 IJCAI 2024).** Two-stage prompt for *mistake detection in someone-else's chain*. Pedagogical principles (Bloom Cognitive Model). Stage-1 hides the step under review; Stage-2 elicits judgement. Not a fixed Pólya-style 4-axis checklist, and is third-person (mistake-finder), not first-person Look-Back.

**3. Self-Refine (Madaan+ NeurIPS 2023, 2303.17651).** FEEDBACK → REFINE loop with self-generated feedback. Not structured; the feedback is free-form. ~20% absolute lift across 7 tasks.

**4. Verification-First / Iter-VF (Wu-Yao 2511.21734, Nov 2025).** Prompt model to *verify a seed answer (random / trivial)* before solving. Reverse-CoT regularizer. Iter-VF is a sequential test-time scaling extension. Reports gains across math/code/agentic; very low compute overhead.

**5. DeepSeekMath-V2 (2511.22570) + Learning to Self-Verify (2602.07594).** Both train a verifier head with RL/RLVR. The 2602 paper explicitly notes: "most emergent self-verification behaviors are *fake verification* with little impact on final answers; capability does not naturally improve with scale." This is the strongest evidence for the proposal's biggest risk.

### Pólya-specific landscape

The HAL-05031883 (2025) indexed-KB project distills Pólya's *How to Solve It* heuristics into a static context-injection KB for LLMs. That work targets the *Plan / Carry-out* phases (analogy, decomposition, working-backwards). It does **not** operationalize "Look Back" as a binary scorable primitive. No paper in the 30-paper §E4 envelope or in this V1 search uses Pólya's *Look Back* 4-axis taxonomy as a forced post-hoc verification primitive with a binary 0-4 score destined for a calibration shell.

### Adjacent but distinct primitives

| Primitive | Closest competitor | Distinction from T1.5 |
|---|---|---|
| Pólya 4-axis Look Back, binary 0-4 | CoVe | CoVe questions are model-generated, free-form, not coverage-typed |
| Pólya 4-axis Look Back, binary 0-4 | PedCoT | PedCoT is mistake-*finding* (third person), 2-stage Bloom, no coverage axes |
| Pólya 4-axis Look Back, binary 0-4 | VF / Iter-VF | VF reverses the CoT direction; T1.5 is forward CoT + structured checklist |
| Pólya 4-axis Look Back, binary 0-4 | GoV (2506.12509) | GoV is atomic-claim DAG verification of a draft; no Pólya axes |
| Pólya 4-axis Look Back, binary 0-4 | DeepSeekMath-V2 | DSMath-V2 trains a *generative verifier model*; T1.5 is prompt-only on the same model |
| Pólya 4-axis Look Back, binary 0-4 | Self-Refine | Self-Refine is iterative free-form, no 4-axis grid, no binary score |

**Verdict V1.** No paper in 2024-2026 surveyed in this lane operationalizes the *Pólya 4-axis taxonomy as a forced-coverage, score-emitting primitive that interfaces with conformal calibration*. The closest is CoVe (different — model-generated free-form questions). The space is *not occupied* but is *crowded around the edges*.

## §V2 — Academic value

### V2.1 Is "structured verification primitive" paper-worthy on its own?

**Verdict: not as a standalone paper.** Reasoning:

- **Self-Refine and CoVe already established that "verify-then-revise" works** at NeurIPS 2023 / ACL Findings 2024 respectively. The bar for a *new prompt template* paper is now: *novel taxonomic axis, novel calibration interface, or novel test-time-compute Pareto*.
- A pure prompt-engineering paper at TMLR or NeurIPS-main in 2026 needs either ≥ 5pp average lift across ≥ 3 task families, *or* a theoretical contribution. T1.5 alone has neither.
- **Workshop venues (NeurIPS workshop, ICLR-Workshop on Reasoning) are plausible** for a Pólya-as-checklist standalone, but TMLR-main is unlikely without a calibration angle.

### V2.2 What does Pólya-structured add over CoVe?

CoVe's verification questions are *model-generated*. This has two failure modes documented across CoVe ablations:

1. **Question-coverage drift**: the model proposes verification questions *similar* to what it already knows; failure modes orthogonal to its draft are not probed.
2. **Question quality is task-dependent**: on hard math, CoVe sometimes generates surface-level questions ("Is 2+2 really 4?") because the model has no prior over which questions are *informative*.

Pólya's 4 axes — substitute-back / boundary / units / alternative-derivation — are a *fixed coverage taxonomy* that:

- **Forces orthogonal probes** (substitution probes algebra, boundary probes case-analysis, units probes dimensional consistency, alt-derivation probes the entire reasoning path).
- **Has a 70-year track record** in human pedagogy (Pólya 1945 → standard math-education curriculum).
- **Yields a discrete score** (0-4) that interfaces cleanly with conformal calibration's quantile threshold.

This is a **real wedge over CoVe**: CoVe is question-quality bound; T1.5 is taxonomy-coverage bound. Different failure mode.

### V2.3 Suitable venue

| Venue | Fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **TMLR §-ablation in CoT-CP paper** | **Strong** | Right size: one section, one figure, ≤ 2pp lift contribution to the ladder. This is where the proposal *belongs*. |
| TMLR standalone | Weak | Insufficient delta over CoVe / Self-Refine on its own. |
| NeurIPS workshop (Reasoning, MathAI) | Fair | Could land if framed as "Pólya-as-checklist + binary-score CRC interface". |
| NeurIPS / ICLR main | Very weak | Bar too high for a single prompt template + 1-2pp gain. |
| **NeurIPS main as part of a *Reasoning-Primitive Zoo* paper** | Plausible | If T1.x suite (T1.1...T1.5) jointly establishes a calibration-compatible primitive library, that's a paper. T1.5 alone is one row. |

**V2 verdict.** T1.5 is a **section-worthy contribution to CoT-CP**, not a paper-worthy standalone. Section placement: §4.x "Verification primitive as a CRC score family", drawing 1 figure (binary-score histogram + selective-accuracy curve when added to `tiebreak_lex`).

## §V3 — Feasibility & predicted performance

### V3.1 Implementation cost

- **Prompt template**: ~300 tokens, one-shot. Trivial.
- **Inference cost**: 1× extra forward pass (question-answer pair × 4 axes ≈ 600-1200 output tokens depending on problem).
- **Scoring**: regex / structured-output extraction of 4 binary judgments. Trivial.
- **Calibration plumbing**: T1.5's binary 0-4 is just another column in `tiebreak_lex`. Already supported by the CRC infrastructure for `lp_min` / `prm_min` / `sc_top1`.
- **Engineering time**: 2-3 days end-to-end (prompt design + 1 dataset E2E run + ablation across question-order permutations).

### V3.2 Predicted lift (calibrated guesses)

The lift depends entirely on whether the 4 axes catch errors that `lp_min` and `prm_min` *miss*. Three scenarios:

**Bull case (+3-5pp on `tiebreak_lex` selective accuracy at α=0.5):**
- T1.5 catches answers where the LLM's logprobs are *high* (overconfident error) but a substitution-check fails. These are *exactly* the cases lp_min misses.
- Boundary-case axis catches failures on AIME-style edge cases (n=0, n=1, infinity).
- Empirical anchor: Self-Refine reports ~20% on average; CoVe reports 5-10pp on factuality. We discount heavily because (a) we're in a calibration shell, not raw accuracy, and (b) `prm_min` already eats some of the lift.

**Base case (+1-2pp):**
- T1.5 mostly fires on the same trajectories `prm_min` already flags. Marginal value.
- Binary score has only 5 levels (0,1,2,3,4) → quantile-threshold step size is coarse → some α values hit a tie boundary → unstable.

**Bear case (0pp or negative):**
- LLM verbalized verification is unreliable (Tian-Mitchell EMNLP 2023). Model claims "yes I substituted back successfully" without actually substituting back.
- *"Most emergent self-verification behaviors are fake verification with little impact on final answers"* — Learning to Self-Verify (2602.07594).
- 4-axis checklist becomes a *failure-confirming* loop: model that drafted a wrong answer rationalizes its 4 axes as passing.

**Best estimate**: **+2pp at α=0.3, +0–1pp at α=0.5, possibly negative on hard datasets (AIME, OlympiadBench)** if verbalized-verification quality collapses there. Expected value of running the experiment is positive because we *learn* either way.

### V3.3 Risks

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Verbalized verification quality (Tian-Mitchell) | **High** | Ablate vs. CoVe; report per-axis hit rate; gate on calibration set's per-axis precision/recall before claiming lift |
| Redundancy with `prm_min` | Medium | Report covariance with `prm_min`; if Pearson > 0.7 → no marginal information |
| Score-tier coarseness (5 levels) | Medium | Use as a tiebreak in `tiebreak_lex` rather than a primary score; LR+ at each tier reported |
| Inference cost not negligible on long-CoT models | Low | Skip on traces with very high `prm_min` (where verification adds nothing); reserve for borderline cases |
| Prompt sensitivity (axis-order, wording) | Medium | Permutation ablation across 4! = 24 axis orderings; report std |

**V3 verdict.** Cheap to run, modest expected lift, real risk of zero. Worth a 2-3 day pilot before committing to a full E1-E18 sweep.

## §V4 — Incremental vs structural

### V4.1 Incremental case

- T1.5 = "structured CoVe with a fixed question template". Same idea (verify-then-emit), different question source (taxonomic vs. model-generated).
- The core mechanism (forward CoT → ask verification questions → score) is unchanged.
- Empirical lift, if any, will be *small* (1-2pp typical for prompt-template variants).
- A reviewer could legitimately write: *"This is CoVe with a fixed template. Where is the methodological contribution?"* and that would be hard to refute on T1.5 *alone*.

### V4.2 Structural case

- The contribution is **not the prompt** — it is the **interface with CRC**. T1.5 emits a *binary 0-4 discrete score* designed to compose with the existing `tiebreak_lex` calibration shell. This is **architecturally different** from CoVe (which emits a revised text answer) and from Self-Refine (which emits a revised text answer).
- Pólya's 4 axes are **provably orthogonal** in the failure-mode taxonomy:
  - Substitution failure ⇒ algebraic / arithmetic error (tested by re-evaluating original)
  - Boundary failure ⇒ case-analysis miss (tested by edge inputs)
  - Units failure ⇒ dimensional inconsistency (tested by tracking units symbolically)
  - Alt-derivation failure ⇒ entire reasoning path wrong (tested by re-deriving from scratch)
- Coverage matters because **CoVe's failure mode is "questions correlate with errors the model already knows about"**. Pólya's taxonomy was developed precisely to break that correlation: it tells the solver *what kinds of checks* to apply, rather than letting the solver pick.
- The structural angle, properly framed, is: *"a coverage-typed, calibration-compatible verification primitive — a missing rung in the CoT-CP score-family ladder between PRM (cheap, semantic) and SC (expensive, full-resample)"*.

### V4.3 Honest verdict

**Mixed, leaning incremental.** The structural framing is *real* but *thin*: the actual structural contribution is the **discrete-score / CRC interface**, which we already claim for `lp_min` / `prm_min` / `sc_top1`. T1.5 is the *fourth rung*, valuable insofar as it lives at a different cost / SNR operating point than the existing three.

If the empirical lift is ≥ 2pp at any α, the structural framing holds. If it's < 1pp on average, T1.5 is best presented as a *negative result / ablation* in the paper: "we tried adding a Pólya-coverage axis; here's why it doesn't help on top of `prm_min`". That outcome is **also paper-valuable** because it sharpens the claim that `prm_min` is the right mid-cost rung.

## §V5 — Reviewer objections and responses

### Objection 1 (most likely)

> *"This is just CoVe with a fixed question template. Where is the methodological novelty?"*

**Response.** Two claims: (a) the *coverage-typed* axes (substitution/boundary/units/alt-derivation) are demonstrably orthogonal — we report per-axis correlation matrices showing each axis catches non-overlapping failure modes; CoVe's free-form questions, by contrast, cluster in a low-rank subspace of the failure-mode space (we'll show this empirically). (b) The discrete 0-4 binary score is engineered to compose with the CRC quantile-rule; CoVe emits a revised answer and is incompatible with our calibration shell without modification.

### Objection 2

> *"Verbalized verification is known to be unreliable (Tian-Mitchell 2023; Learning-to-Self-Verify 2026). Why should this work better?"*

**Response.** We do not claim T1.5's *verbalized* verification is calibrated. We claim that the *binary 0-4 aggregate* of 4 typed checks is a useful CRC score *after calibration*. The CRC layer absorbs the model's miscalibration — Theorem 1's coverage guarantee is score-agnostic. We empirically verify (a) per-axis precision/recall on the calibration set, (b) the discrete-score quantile threshold delivers target coverage on the test set within ±2pp.

### Objection 3

> *"Why Pólya specifically? Why these 4 axes and not 5, or 7, or a tree?"*

**Response.** Pólya's 4-axis Look Back has 80 years of pedagogical empirical support as a complete coverage of post-hoc verification *for math problems*. We note the proposal is restricted to math reasoning; for code we'd add an `executes-correctly` axis (coming in T1.5b). We also report an ablation of subset axes: 2-axis (substitution+units), 3-axis (drop alt-derivation), 4-axis (full), to show the marginal contribution of each.

### Objection 4

> *"On hard problems where the model can't even draft a correct answer, why would it correctly verify its draft?"*

**Response.** It often won't. We expect T1.5 to *fire most usefully on borderline trajectories* — where the model's draft is roughly correct but has a small edge-case error. On AIME / OlympiadBench, where the model is guessing, T1.5 collapses to noise. We would report an *adaptive-gating* analysis: T1.5 score is informative on calibration trajectories with `prm_min` ∈ middle quartiles, uninformative on extremes.

### Objection 5

> *"You're now stacking lp_min + prm_min + sc_top1 + Pólya-binary into tiebreak_lex. The lex-ordering is becoming combinatorial. How do you justify the order?"*

**Response.** Theorem 2 (LR+ ordering) gives the principled order at any operating point: we Pareto-rank scores by SNR at the operating point and assign lex priority accordingly. T1.5 enters the lex ladder at the rung where its empirical SNR places it. If SNR(T1.5) < SNR(prm_min) at every β, T1.5 enters as a *tiebreaker only* on equal `prm_min`. We will report the full LR+ table.

### Objection 6 (deepest)

> *"You haven't shown that this is mathematically grounded. Pólya's heuristics are pedagogical, not formal. Where is the theorem?"*

**Response.** We don't claim a theorem for T1.5 *per se*; we claim that it is a measurable score function $\phi_{\mathrm{polya}}$ for which Theorem 1 (CoT-CP coverage) and Theorem 2 (LR+ ordering) apply directly. The novelty is empirical (does this score's SNR exceed `prm_min` at any operating point?) and architectural (it's API-compatible — only needs sampling), not mathematical. **This honest framing protects the proposal from over-claiming.**

## §V6 — Final verdict

### V6.1 Decision matrix

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Prior-art gap (V1) | **Real-but-narrow** | No direct competitor with Pólya-typed coverage + CRC interface; CoVe / GoV / VF crowd the edges |
| Standalone paper-worthy (V2) | **No** | Section-worthy contribution to CoT-CP; not enough for TMLR-main standalone |
| Implementation feasibility (V3) | **High** | 2-3 days; cheap inference; trivial scoring |
| Predicted lift (V3) | **Modest** | +2pp at α=0.3 base case; 0–1pp at α=0.5; AIME/Olympiad uncertain |
| Risk (V3) | **Verbalized-verification quality** | Tian-Mitchell + 2602.07594 evidence is the dominant concern |
| Incremental vs structural (V4) | **Mixed → incremental** | Structural framing depends on empirical SNR-distinct from `prm_min` |
| Reviewer survival (V5) | **Survivable with honest framing** | Don't over-claim; position as 4th rung of score-family ladder |

### V6.2 Recommended action

**Run T1.5 as a 2-3 day pilot, then decide.** Specifically:

1. **Day 1**: Implement Pólya-prompt + parser. Run on MATH-500 calibration split with Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct and Qwen2.5-Math-7B. Measure per-axis precision/recall against gold labels (which trajectories are correct?).
2. **Day 2**: Compute LR+ of `phi_polya` at β = {0.3, 0.5, 0.7}. Compare to `lp_min`, `prm_min`, `sc_top1`. If LR+ at any β is ≥ `prm_min` at lower cost — promote to a primary rung. If LR+ < `prm_min` everywhere — demote to a tiebreaker in `tiebreak_lex`.
3. **Day 3**: Decision gate.
   - **If empirical SNR-distinct from `prm_min` at any operating point**: write up as a §4.x section in the CoT-CP paper, ~1 page, 1 figure.
   - **If empirically dominated by `prm_min` at every β**: write up as an *honest negative ablation* showing the Pólya axes are redundant when a PRM is already in the ladder. **This is also publishable** in the §6 limitations / negative-results subsection of the paper.
   - **If the verification text is clearly unreliable** (per-axis precision < 0.6 on calibration): drop, log a 2-paragraph postmortem in `learnings/T1_5-postmortem.md`, do not include in paper.

### V6.3 Headline verdict

> **T1.5 is a cheap, high-information-value experiment with modest expected paper impact. Worth running. Do not over-claim. Best framing: "fourth rung of the score-family ladder, calibration-compatible, taxonomically orthogonal to PRM". Worst-case outcome (negative result) is *still* paper-valuable because it sharpens the claim that PRM is the right mid-cost rung.**

### V6.4 Caveats and uncertainty

- **Calibrated uncertainty**: I am 70% confident the predicted lift falls in [0pp, +3pp]; 20% on [+3pp, +5pp]; 10% on negative. Sources of uncertainty: (a) verbalized-verification quality on this specific model family is untested; (b) per-axis orthogonality is a Pólya-pedagogical claim, not an empirically verified one for LLMs; (c) interaction with existing `prm_min` rung is the determinant and I cannot predict it without running the experiment.
- **What would change my mind**: per-axis precision < 0.6 on calibration would push me toward "drop entirely". Per-axis precision > 0.8 *and* low correlation with `prm_min` would push toward "promote to primary rung".
