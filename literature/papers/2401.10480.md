## Abstract (verbatim)

Self-consistency (SC) has been a widely used decoding strategy for
chain-of-thought reasoning. Despite bringing significant performance
improvements across a variety of multi-step reasoning tasks, it is a
high-cost method that requires multiple sampling with the preset size. In
this paper, we propose a simple and scalable sampling process, Early-Stopping
Self-Consistency (ESC), to greatly reduce the cost of SC without sacrificing
performance. On this basis, one control scheme for ESC is further derivated
to dynamically choose the performance-cost balance for different tasks and
models. To demonstrate ESC's effectiveness, we conducted extensive
experiments on three popular categories of reasoning tasks (arithmetic,
commonsense and symbolic reasoning) over language models with varying scales.
The empirical results show that ESC reduces the average number of sampling
of chain-of-thought reasoning by a significant margin while attaining
comparable performances to original SC on six benchmarks (MATH, GSM8K,
StrategyQA, CommonsenseQA, Coin Flip, and Last Letters).

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

ESC replaces fixed-N self-consistency with a **sliding-window
zero-entropy** stopping rule: keep sampling CoT traces until *all* answers
in the most recent window of size w agree (i.e. window entropy = 0), then
stop and emit the majority answer over everything sampled so far. Window
size w is the only hyperparameter: paper uses w=8 on MATH and w=5 on the
other five benchmarks, with a control-scheme algorithm (Algorithm 2) that
chooses w from a small initial-sample probe to balance expected cost vs.
expected accuracy. ESC reports 33.8%–84.2% sampling-cost reduction across
six benchmarks at near-zero accuracy drop. The threshold choice (window
size w + the trivial entropy=0 condition) is a heuristic *task-and-model*
parameter — the paper itself frames Algorithm 2 as "choose appropriate
values of (w, L) based on the respective expected values," confirming there
is no calibrated coverage guarantee.

## Method

- **Step-level signal used**: Trace-level final-answer agreement within a
  sliding window of size w. The signal is binary at the window level
  (entropy = 0 iff all w answers identical).
- **Stopping/branching rule**: After each new sample, examine the most
  recent w final answers. **If all w are identical, stop**; else continue.
  Output = majority vote over all samples taken.
- **Threshold tuning**: Window size w is the threshold. Paper uses
  - **w = 8 on MATH** (more difficult, higher answer-entropy → larger
    window needed to avoid premature stopping),
  - **w = 5 on GSM8K, StrategyQA, CommonsenseQA, Coin Flip, Last Letters**.
  Algorithm 2 ("control scheme"): take an initial w_0 = 5 probe, fit a
  per-task expected cost E[cost(w, L)] (Eq. 10) and expected performance
  E[acc(w, L)] (Eq. 14), then pick (w, L) satisfying budget B and
  accuracy P targets. Parameters of the cost/accuracy expectations are
  fit on the probe samples themselves — **no held-out calibration**.
- **Per-prompt vs per-step adaptation**: **Per-prompt** at the
  trace-completion granularity (each sample is a full CoT). w is **fixed
  per (task, model)** pair, not per prompt.

## Reported empirical results (the headline numbers)

- Models: GPT-3.5 / Code-Davinci-002 / LLaMA-class. Benchmarks: MATH,
  GSM8K, StrategyQA, CommonsenseQA, Coin Flip, Last Letters.
- Sampling-cost reduction (averaged): MATH -33.8%, GSM8K -80.1%,
  StrategyQA -76.8%, CommonsenseQA -78.5%, Coin Flip -84.2%, Last Letters
  -67.4%.
- Accuracy: comparable to vanilla SC at the matched budget — the paper
  reports near-zero accuracy delta on all six benchmarks.

## Where their threshold is heuristic (positioning)

- Window size w is the **single tunable hyperparameter** and **must be
  re-picked per (task, model)**. The paper explicitly chooses w=8 for MATH
  and w=5 for the other five benchmarks with no principled calibration —
  Algorithm 2 fits cost/accuracy on the *probe* samples themselves.
- The stopping condition (entropy=0 in the window) is **brittle to
  answer-space cardinality**: open-ended math (MATH, AIME) has answer
  alphabets where consecutive identical answers are weak evidence; ESC
  partially recognizes this by raising w to 8 on MATH but provides no
  finite-sample correctness guarantee.
- The paper's framing — "dynamically choose the performance-cost balance
  for different tasks and models" — is precisely the per-task tuning that
  CoT-CP eliminates.
- Brittleness under distribution shift: change benchmark difficulty (GSM8K
  → AIME) or model strength (GPT-3.5 → Qwen3-8B), the answer-agreement
  curves shift, w must be re-tuned, and accuracy can silently drop.
- No coverage guarantee: ESC's accuracy preservation is empirical
  ("comparable to SC"), not statistical. There is no claim
  P(correct) ≥ 1 − α.

## How CoT-CP positions against this

- **Empirical overlap**: Partial — MATH and GSM8K overlap. AIME-24/25 and
  GPQA-Diamond are not in ESC's original suite but are the natural modern
  extension we will run for both.
- **What CoT-CP adds**:
  - Distribution-free coverage statement P(correct) ≥ 1 − α via CRC; ESC
    has no such guarantee.
  - Removes the per-(task, model) w tuning: CoT-CP's q̂ is calibrated
    once on PRM800K-style data and transfers under exchangeability.
  - **Step-level** instrumentation: ESC must finish each CoT trace; CoT-CP
    can gate at step boundaries inside a trace.
  - Window-zero-entropy is itself a valid score function for CoT-CP —
    "1[window of w final-answers all agree]" can be the conformity score,
    and CoT-CP gives it a calibrated quantile.
  - α-targeted budget rather than (w, L)-tuned budget: user picks
    target accuracy, calibration picks budget.
- **Required experimental baseline**: yes — reproduce ESC with both w=5
  and w=8 on MATH-500, GSM8K, and (extended) AIME-24/25, GPQA-Diamond
  using at least one shared model. Report cost-vs-accuracy curves
  head-to-head.

  Adaptive-Consistency), §3 (Related Work — early-stopping-SC subsection),
  §5 (Experiments — baseline row), §6 (Discussion — ESC as a
  window-entropy special case of CoT-CP).
