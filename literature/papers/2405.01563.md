## Abstract (verbatim)

"We develop a principled procedure for determining when a large language model (LLM) should abstain from responding (e.g., by saying 'I don't know') in a general domain, instead of resorting to possibly 'hallucinating' a non-sensical or incorrect answer. Building on earlier approaches that use self-consistency as a more reliable measure of model confidence, we propose using the LLM itself to self-evaluate the similarity between each of its sampled responses for a given query. We then further leverage conformal prediction techniques to develop an abstention procedure that benefits from rigorous theoretical guarantees on the hallucination rate (error rate). Experimentally, our resulting conformal abstention method reliably bounds the hallucination rate on various closed-book, open-domain generative question answering datasets, while also maintaining a significantly less conservative abstention rate on a dataset with long responses (Temporal Sequences) compared to baselines using log-probability scores to quantify uncertainty, while achieving comparable performance on a dataset with short answers (TriviaQA). To evaluate the experiments automatically, one needs to determine if two responses are equivalent given a question. Following standard practice, we use a thresholded similarity function to determine if two responses match, but also provide a method for calibrating the threshold based on conformal prediction, with theoretical guarantees on the accuracy of the match prediction, which might be of independent interest."

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

Abbasi-Yadkori et al. give the cleanest "CP for selective LLM answering" recipe to date: sample N responses, have the *LLM itself* self-evaluate pairwise similarity to produce a self-consistency-based confidence score, then split-CP-calibrate an abstention threshold so the *hallucination rate* (error rate among non-abstaining answers) is bounded by alpha with finite-sample guarantees. They additionally calibrate the equivalence-judgment threshold via a secondary CP step, providing accuracy guarantees on the auto-evaluator itself. The construction is the closest direct precedent for trajectory-level CP-based abstention, and is *the* paper CoT-CP must be benchmarked against because their `sc_top1`-style score family is essentially what we instantiate as one rung of our score-family ladder.

## Method
- **Conformity score**: self-consistency frequency derived from N sampled responses, with pairwise similarity computed by the LLM itself (a self-eval prompt). Concretely: an aggregated agreement score over the N samples that correlates with correctness.
- **Calibration set**: held-out (X, Y) pairs with binary correctness; split CP threshold computed on the empirical loss distribution.
- **Coverage statement**: marginal P[error | not-abstain] <= alpha (hallucination rate bound). A second CP layer bounds the accuracy of the LLM-as-judge similarity threshold itself.
- **Granularity**: query-level abstain/answer decision (no per-claim or per-step decomposition).

## Reported results
- Datasets: closed-book open-domain QA (TriviaQA short-answer; Temporal Sequences long-answer); other QA benchmarks per the camera-ready.
- Models: LLM-based (concrete model list in the paper; typical PaLM/Gemini-family given the DeepMind authorship).
- Headline: bounded hallucination rate on TriviaQA and Temporal Sequences; *significantly lower* abstention rate than log-probability-based baselines on long-form responses while matching on short-answer.
- This is the empirical anchor paper for "self-consistency + CP beats log-prob + CP" — directly relevant to our `sc_top1 vs lp_min` Pareto results.

## How CoT-CP positions against this
- **Overlap with CoT-CP**: very high. Both use self-consistency-as-conformity to drive an abstain/keep decision via split CP, and both target a marginal selective-error guarantee. Their `sc_top1`-style score is essentially identical to one of our three score families (`sc_top1`, the N-sample majority share). On QA-style tasks, CoT-CP and Abbasi-Yadkori produce algorithmically very similar predictors at the trajectory level.
- **What CoT-CP adds**:
  - Score-family Pareto (Theorem 2). Abbasi-Yadkori commit to self-consistency as the score and do not study cheaper alternatives. CoT-CP shows lp_min (1x), prm_min (2x), sc_top1 (8x or 16x) form an LR+-ordered Pareto frontier in (compute, selective accuracy). PRM as a *new mid-cost operating point* — 2/3 of SC@8 lift at 1/4 the compute (MATH-500: prm_min α=0.5 → 70.7% kept-acc on 38% kept vs sc_top1 α=0.5 → 79.3% on 49% kept) — is a contribution they cannot make.
  - Reasoning-domain breadth. Abbasi-Yadkori test on QA (TriviaQA, Temporal Sequences). CoT-CP tests on math reasoning (MATH-500, GSM8K, OlympiadBench, AIME, MMLU-Pro STEM, TheoremQA) plus HumanEval code, across 11 models including reasoning-finetuned (R1-Distill-32B/70B, QwQ-32B), MoE (Qwen3-30B-A3B), and math-specialized (Qwen2.5-Math-7B).
  - Step-level structure. Even though our headline result is trajectory-level, we explicitly study and *negatively report* step-level branching (Pilots C/K/L/M show ≤ +1pt over trajectory CP). Abbasi-Yadkori never decompose the response. Our null result is a contribution: it falsifies the natural "smarter abstention via per-step replanning" extension of their framework.
  - Distribution shift. CoT-CP Theorem 3 + Section 2.6 (MATH→AIME) targets cal/test shift on discrete scores via empirical-PMF weighted CP. Abbasi-Yadkori assume IID cal/test.
  - Selective-accuracy as primary metric. Abbasi-Yadkori bound the hallucination rate among non-abstainers (marginal error). CoT-CP reports kept-accuracy + bootstrap 95% CIs across the full alpha grid for direct comparison with vanilla / SC ladders, which is a more demanding empirical commitment.
- **Required experimental baseline**: yes — this is the *primary* CP baseline. Implement their pipeline at matched N (8 samples) on MATH-500, OlympiadBench, AIME using the same Qwen2.5-Math-7B / Qwen3-8B-no-think generators. Their method *is* effectively our `sc_top1` rung at trajectory level, so we should label the `sc_top1` row in our headline table as "= Abbasi-Yadkori 2024" and emphasize that lp_min and prm_min rungs are *new* below their operating point.
- **Required citation**: Section 2 (Related Work, abstention CP — co-anchor with Mohri-Hashimoto), Section 3 (Methods; explicit equivalence between our `sc_top1` rung and their score), Section 5 (Results; label headline rows accordingly), Section 6 (Discussion; acknowledge they are the direct precedent and that our score-family ladder is the genuine extension).
