## Abstract (verbatim)

> In this paper, we present an innovative process-oriented math process reward model called Math-Shepherd, which assigns a reward score to each step of math problem solutions. The training of Math-Shepherd is achieved using automatically constructed process-wise supervision data, breaking the bottleneck of heavy reliance on manual annotation in existing work. We explore the effectiveness of Math-Shepherd in two scenarios: 1) Verification: Math-Shepherd is utilized for reranking multiple outputs generated by Large Language Models (LLMs); 2) Reinforcement Learning: Math-Shepherd is employed to reinforce LLMs with step-by-step Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO). With Math-Shepherd, a series of open-source LLMs demonstrates exceptional performance. For instance, the step-by-step PPO with Math-Shepherd significantly improves the accuracy of Mistral-7B (77.9% → 84.1% on GSM8K and 28.6% → 33.0% on MATH). The accuracy can be further enhanced to 89.1% and 43.5% on GSM8K and MATH with the verification of Math-Shepherd, respectively. We believe that automatic process supervision holds significant potential for the future evolution of LLMs.

## Key idea (3-5 sentences)

Math-Shepherd answers PRM800K's "where does the human-annotation labor come from?" by replacing humans with Monte Carlo rollouts: from any prefix $s_{1:t}$, the *empirical probability* that a completer LLM finishes with the correct gold answer is taken as the step label.
Two label variants are studied — *Hard Estimation* (binary: any rollout reaches gold ⇒ 1) and *Soft Estimation* (fraction of rollouts that reach gold).
The resulting auto-labeled dataset trains a PRM that is then used both for best-of-N verification re-ranking and as a dense step reward inside step-level PPO.
With Mistral-7B as the base, the combination raises GSM8K from 77.9% → 89.1% and MATH from 28.6% → 43.5%, demonstrating that human-free process supervision can match or beat Lightman-style human-annotated PRMs *on the same in-distribution problems*.
The crucial caveat (made explicit by ProcessBench, §B4 in the litreview) is that the auto-labeled signal does not survive distribution shift: Math-Shepherd-PRM-7B is the *worst* PRM on Olympiad-level problems among published open PRMs.

## Method
- **What is computed at each step**: An empirical estimate of $P(\text{final answer correct} \mid \text{prefix} = s_{1:t})$ using $N$ Monte Carlo rollouts of a fixed *completer* policy from $s_{1:t}$.
- **Training data**: Auto-generated. For each (problem $P$, partial solution $s_{1:t}$), sample $N$ continuations from a completer LLM, extract their final answers $\{a_1, \dots, a_N\}$, compare against gold $a^\star$.
- **Score function definition**:
  - **Hard Estimation (HE)**: $y^{\text{HE}}_{s_{1:t}} = \mathbb{1}\!\left[\exists\, j \in [N]:\ a_j = a^\star\right]$ — 1 iff at least one of $N$ rollouts reaches the gold answer.
  - **Soft Estimation (SE)**: $y^{\text{SE}}_{s_{1:t}} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{j=1}^{N} \mathbb{1}[a_j = a^\star]$ — empirical success probability.
  - The PRM is trained to regress these labels (cross-entropy for HE, MSE for SE) on prefix-conditioned representations of the base LM.
  - Default: **N = 8** completions per step in the main Math-Shepherd training; ablations from $N = 4$ up to $N = 256$ in verification experiments. Completer: **LLemma-7B** for the main run; LLaMA-2-70B + MetaMATH for stronger-completer ablation. The labeling cost scales as $\mathcal{O}(N \cdot T \cdot |\mathcal{D}|)$ where $T$ is the average trace length.
- **Aggregation to final**: Trace score for verification re-ranking is min (or product) of per-step scores. For PPO, per-step rewards are used directly as a dense advantage signal in step-level PPO (one update per reasoning step rather than per trace).

## Reported results

| Base | Method | GSM8K | MATH |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mistral-7B | SFT (baseline) | 77.9 | 28.6 |
| Mistral-7B | + Step-PPO (Math-Shepherd) | 84.1 | 33.0 |
| Mistral-7B | + Step-PPO + Verification | **89.1** | **43.5** |
| LLaMA-2-70B-MetaMATH | + Verification (HE, N=4) | label-quality study reports HE label accuracy ≈ 86% | — |
| DeepSeek-67B | + Verification | substantial gains over ORM (Tab. 3) | — |

- **HE vs SE**: HE is more robust under label noise; SE is finer but suffers when $N$ is small.
- **Step-level PPO** outperforms outcome PPO and ORM-rerank baselines.
- **Verification scaling**: best-of-N continues to improve up to $N = 256$.
- **Generalization caveat**: ProcessBench (§B4) later shows Math-Shepherd-PRM-7B drops from 47.9 F1 on GSM8K to 24.8 / 23.8 on Olympiad / Omni-MATH — the auto-label signal is brittle outside training distribution.
- **Implicit assumption**: gold answer $a^\star$ is verifiable, which limits Math-Shepherd to math / code / verifiable domains. Free-form CoT (e.g., commonsense, GPQA-Diamond) needs a different labeling pipeline.

## How CoT-CP uses this paper
- **Role**: **Candidate score function** $s_t$ and **auto-labeled calibration alternative** to PRM800K.
- **Specific use**:
  - The MC-rollout completion success rate $y^{\text{SE}}$ is one of the candidate non-conformity scores in CoT-CP. It is *literally an empirical probability*, which is convenient for CRC because the calibration loss can be directly framed as a coverage statement.
  - Math-Shepherd-style auto-labeling is the fallback when PRM800K-style human labels do not exist for a benchmark (e.g., for AIME calibration).
  - We use it as a **second calibration source** for ablation studies — replacing PRM800K labels with MC-rollout labels and asking whether CoT-CP coverage transfers.
  - We do **not** use Math-Shepherd's PPO training side; CoT-CP is inference-time.
- **What CoT-CP adds on top**:
  - Math-Shepherd scores are point estimates with no coverage guarantee. ProcessBench (B4) shows Math-Shepherd-PRM-7B is the *worst* PRM on Olympiad subsets (24.8 / 23.8 F1), badly underperforming outside training distribution.
  - CoT-CP wraps the score with a calibrated quantile $\hat{q}$ so the threshold is data-driven rather than tuned, and the resulting accept/reject step gate enjoys distribution-free finite-sample coverage.
- **Required citation**: §3.1 (label generation), §3.2 (HE vs SE definitions), §4 (PPO and verification results, especially Table 1, 2 for Mistral-7B numbers).

## Limitations (what this paper does *not* deliver)
- No coverage / calibration guarantee on the PRM output; trained as a regressor on noisy MC labels.
- $y^{\text{SE}}$ depends on completer policy; switching the completer at inference time invalidates the trained PRM.
- Limited to verifiable-answer domains (math, code) — does not extend to free-form reasoning where $a_j = a^\star$ check is undefined.
- $N = 8$ rollouts is empirically chosen; no theoretical analysis of label-noise propagation.
- ProcessBench shows the auto-labeled PRM does *not* generalize beyond GSM8K/MATH-style problems — the brittleness is severe.

## Open questions for CoT-CP design
1. With $N = 8$ rollouts, $y^{\text{SE}}$ has Monte-Carlo standard error $\sigma_{\text{MC}} \approx \sqrt{p(1-p)/N} \le 1/(2\sqrt{N}) \approx 0.18$ at $p = 0.5$. Does this label noise propagate into the calibrated quantile $\hat{q}$?
   - Plausibly yes; calibration set should be large enough that the sampling noise is dominated by the calibration set's own finite-sample slack.
   - Mitigation: use only steps with $y^{\text{SE}} \in \{0, 1\}$ (high-confidence labels) for calibration.
2. Can we use Math-Shepherd labels for *AIME / GPQA-Diamond* calibration where PRM800K-style human labels do not exist?
   - CRC's distribution-free guarantee gives this for free if the rollouts are exchangeable with test-time prompts.
   - Caveat: GPQA gold answers are MCQ; rollout-success is noisy because guessing succeeds 25% of the time.
3. Replacing the Math-Shepherd PRM head with raw $y^{\text{SE}}$ (no neural head) eliminates one source of miscalibration but costs $N$ rollouts per step at inference. Trade-off study needed.
4. PAVs (§B6) argue $y^{\text{SE}}$ is *not* a good RL reward signal but say nothing about its calibration properties for CP — possibly $y^{\text{SE}}$ is a *better* score for CP than for RL. Worth a side-by-side ablation.
